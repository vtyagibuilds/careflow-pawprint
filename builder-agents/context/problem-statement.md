# Problem Statement

## The Customer Problem

When a pet owner experiences a significant life moment — bringing home a new puppy, losing a dog, a pet's first birthday — they are in a heightened emotional and practical state. They have urgent needs: what to buy, what to do, how to prepare. They are actively looking for guidance.

The retailer is present in this moment. The customer is already in contact — placing an order, reaching out to support. But the retailer responds generically. No acknowledgment of the moment. No relevant guidance. A missed opportunity to be genuinely useful.

The customer moves on. The moment passes. The chance to build real loyalty is gone.

---

## Why It Matters

The first weeks of pet ownership are when brand loyalty is formed. A first-time puppy owner who feels supported and guided by a retailer in week one is far more likely to return — for food, for vet supplies, for grooming, for years.

Generic email marketing cannot capture this. It doesn't know the moment the customer is in. It sends the same promotions to Elizabeth (overwhelmed first-time puppy owner) and to someone whose dog is a healthy 5-year-old. The message lands flat for both.

Lifecycle-triggered personalization is the gap. Detecting the moment, understanding what the customer needs in that moment, and responding with relevance — that is what Pawprint is built to do.

---

## The Specific Problem We Are Solving

A customer contacts support. During the conversation, they reveal a lifecycle signal — a moment that changes what they need. That signal is buried in the chat transcript and acted on by no one.

Pawprint reads the transcript after the conversation closes, identifies the signal, and automatically generates a personalized care package — an email with relevant content, product recommendations, and practical guidance — timed to the moment the customer is actually in.

---

## What We Are Not Solving

- **Generic email marketing** — Pawprint is triggered by lifecycle signals, not by purchase behavior or time-based campaigns
- **Real-time intervention** — the MVP acts after the support chat closes, not during it
- **All lifecycle events at once** — MVP is scoped to one lifecycle event: first-time puppy owner
- **All channels** — MVP delivers by email only
- **Non-dog pets** — dogs only for MVP
- **Customers who don't signal** — Pawprint only acts when a signal is detected; it does not infer or assume

---

## The Measure of Success

A customer in a lifecycle moment receives an email that feels like it was written for them — not because they clicked something or filled out a form, but because the retailer was paying attention. The email is relevant, timely, and useful enough that the customer feels seen.

At MVP scale: Elizabeth gets the email. It doesn't feel automated. She engages with it.
